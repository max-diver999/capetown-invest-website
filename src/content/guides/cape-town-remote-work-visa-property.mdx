## Quick answer: can you combine a remote work visa with Cape Town property?

Yes, but only as two parallel plans. The Remote Work Visitor Visa is an immigration permit for foreign nationals who earn from outside South Africa while living here. Cape Town property is a separate asset you can buy, rent, or let with no visa required and no visa granted by purchase alone.

If your goal is to work remotely from Sea Point or the City Bowl for one to three years, you apply for the remote work route on income and employer evidence. If you also want a home, you follow the normal foreign-buyer path through FICA, exchange control, and conveyancing. Owning does not open the visa, and the visa does not automatically improve bond terms for non-residents. Rules change; treat every figure here as indicative and confirm live requirements with Home Affairs or a registered immigration lawyer before you sign an offer or submit an application.

For the ownership side, start with the [foreigner buying hub](/guides/buy-cape-town-property-foreigner/). For the residency myth, read [does buying property give residency in South Africa](/guides/does-buying-property-give-residency-south-africa/).

## What is the Remote Work Visitor Visa?

South Africa added a formal remote work visitor category when updated Immigration Regulations were gazetted in May 2024, with practical rollout through 2024 and 2025. The category appears under Section 11(1)(b)(iv) of the Immigration Act regulations framework: a visitor permit variant for people whose economic activity remains outside the country.

Colloquially buyers call it a digital nomad visa. Home Affairs language is narrower: you are a visitor who may perform remote work for foreign entities, not a worker sponsored by a South African employer. That distinction controls what you may do on the ground.

The permit suits software engineers, consultants, designers, and other professionals billing EU, UK, US, or other offshore clients from Cape Town. Cape Town's appeal for that profile is practical: UTC+2 aligns with European and UK business hours with only a one-hour gap to Central Europe in winter, English is widely used in business, fibre coverage is strong in core suburbs, and lifestyle density on the Atlantic Seaboard and in the City Bowl is a daily benefit rather than a holiday poster.

This guide focuses on how that visa interacts with property decisions. It is not legal advice. Immigration and tax rules shift; consult a registered immigration practitioner and a cross-border tax adviser for your passport and employer structure.

## Who qualifies for income, employer, and client rules on the remote work visa?

Applicants need gross annual income of at least R650,796 from a foreign employer or foreign client per DIRCO checklist guidance from February 2025, three months of bank statements reflecting stable credits, employment or contracts with entities outside South Africa only, and visitor-class medical cover plus character documents, because local payroll or Cape Town startup contracts fail the foreign-employer test.

Home Affairs and the Department of Home Affairs immigration checklist circulated with DIRCO guidance in February 2025 set out the core financial test remote workers quote most often.

### Minimum income

Applicants must show gross annual income of at least R650,796 from a foreign employer or foreign client relationship. You also supply three months of bank statements that reflect income at a level consistent with that threshold. Officers may ask for employment contracts, invoices, or client letters to corroborate the flow.

If you earn in euros, pounds, or dollars, convert using the logic Home Affairs applies at application time. A foreign salary that comfortably exceeds the rand equivalent on today's rate can still fail if statements do not show stable credits.

### Foreign employer or client only

The visa exists so South Africa captures lifestyle and spend from remote professionals without absorbing local jobs. You must be employed by, or contracted to, an entity outside South Africa. You may not:

- Take employment with a South African company as your primary activity.
- Perform work aimed at the local market that would normally require a work visa.
- Use the permit to compete with resident job seekers in breach of permit conditions.

Freelancers with multiple offshore clients generally fit if the economic relationship stays abroad. A single contract with a Cape Town startup does not. If your employer opens a local branch and wants you on payroll here, that is a work-visa conversation, not remote visitor.

### Health, character, and visitor compliance

Like other visitor-class routes, expect requirements for comprehensive medical cover valid in South Africa, police clearance from countries where you have lived, passport validity, and biometrics per current VFS or Home Affairs practice. Previous overstays or refusals can block approval regardless of income.

South Africa's Remote Work Visitor Visa under Section 11(1)(b)(iv) requires gross annual income of at least R650,796 from a foreign employer or client per DIRCO checklist guidance from February 2025, plus three months of bank statements showing stable credits at application. Permits issue for one year with renewals toward about three years total, and each renewal re-tests income, comprehensive medical cover valid in South Africa, and the prohibition on primary employment with South African companies. Property purchase does not waive those tests or grant the visa, though a lease or deed may corroborate accommodation alongside financial evidence. Foreign buyers may still purchase sectional title after FICA and authorised-dealer capital introduction, with non-resident bond caps near 50 percent of purchase price, meaning a R6 million City Bowl apartment still needs roughly R3 million cash equity regardless of remote salary paid in euros or pounds at mid-2026 rates. Physical presence of 183 days in any rolling 12 months can trigger SARS registration obligations even when the permit category reads visitor class.

## How long you can stay and how renewals work

Published guidance tied to the May 2024 regulations describes a one-year permit with renewals, and total lawful stay commonly up to three years before you must either change category or leave. Each renewal re-tests income, medical cover, and compliance with the no-local-work rule.

Three years is long enough to justify renting first, then buying once you know whether Atlantic Seaboard apartments or City Bowl walk-up living fits your routine. It is also long enough for tax residency questions if you rarely on the ground most of the year. Plan renewals as part of your property timeline: a sectional-title lease may be easier to exit than a sale if your remote contract ends early.

Temporary residence permits under other categories follow different clocks. Do not assume a remote work visitor renewal leads automatically to permanent residence. Permanent residence remains a separate, longer path through qualifying temporary visas or other grounds.

## Property ownership and the visa: separate tracks

South African law keeps immigration and property apart. A foreigner may own Cape Town freehold or sectional-title property with no visa at all, as explained in the [foreigner buying hub](/guides/buy-cape-town-property-foreigner/). Holding a Remote Work Visitor Visa does not change that.

Conversely, buying does not grant the remote work visa. There is no rand purchase threshold that unlocks the permit, unlike golden-visa markets. See [does buying property give residency](/guides/does-buying-property-give-residency-south-africa/) for the broader picture.

What ownership can do is support an application narrative:

- A deed or provisional agreement shows where you intend to live.
- Utility accounts or body-corporate letters show a settled address.
- Combined with bank statements and medical cover, that may help an officer assess genuineness.

What ownership cannot do:

- Waive the R650,796 income test or bank-statement rule.
- Replace foreign-employer evidence.
- Cure a previous overstay.
- Authorise work for a South African employer.

Think of the home as corroboration, not qualification. The qualification remains personal financial and immigration evidence under Section 11(1)(b)(iv).

## Tax: SARS registration and the 183-day rule

Visa label and tax residency are not the same. If you are physically in South Africa for 183 days or more in any rolling 12-month period, you may become tax resident for South African purposes and need to register with SARS. That can affect how offshore salary is reported locally and how rental income on a Cape Town let unit is taxed.

Remote workers splitting time between Europe and Cape Town may remain non-resident for tax if they stay under the day threshold and meet other tests. Count days carefully, including partial days and return trips. A three-year remote visa with most months in Cape Town often pushes people into registration territory.

Property ownership alone does not make you tax resident, but letting triggers rental tax obligations in many cases. Non-resident sellers face withholding on disposal. Cross-border tax advice before you buy or sign a lease is cheap compared with a surprise assessment.

## How does the remote work visa compare to retirement, financially independent, and work visas?

Remote Work Visitor permits rest on foreign employer income of R650,796+, allow no South African employment, and run about three years with renewals, while retired person visas test pension or passive income, financially independent visas test net worth or capital proof, work visas require SA employer sponsorship, and tourist entry allows no work at all regardless of property ownership.

Remote workers sometimes overlap with retirees or financially independent applicants, but the tests differ.

| Route | Core test | Local work allowed? | Typical horizon |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Remote Work Visitor | Foreign employer, R650,796 income floor | No SA employment | Up to about 3 years |
| Retired person's visa | Pension or passive income | No employment | Renewable temporary |
| Financially independent | Net worth or capital proof | No employment | Renewable temporary |
| Work visa | SA employer sponsorship | Yes, with employer | Linked to job |
| Tourist entry | Visitor rules | No work | Short stays |

Property ownership helps none of these categories automatically. It may corroborate ties for retirement or financially independent files more often than for a remote worker who already shows foreign payroll, but it never replaces financial thresholds.

## Costs: visa budget vs property budget

Budget the two separately. Visa costs include Home Affairs or VFS fees, medical examinations, police certificates, translations, practitioner fees, and comprehensive medical aid premiums that meet standards. Those recur on renewal.

Property costs include transfer duty or VAT on new builds, conveyancing, Deeds Office registration, bond costs, rates, and levies. On a R6 million resale apartment, transfer duty alone can reach six figures under the sliding scale modelled in the [cost of buying guide](/guides/cost-of-buying-property-cape-town/).

Non-resident bond caps mean you may need R3 million or more in cash on a R6 million purchase even with a remote worker income in pounds or euros. Visa approval does not change exchange-control lending rules.

## Compliance reminders for remote workers who own property

Introduce purchase funds through an authorised dealer and secure the non-resident endorsement if you buy. Clear FICA before transfer. If you let while abroad, register for tax and use a reputable managing agent.

Keep visitor or remote permit conditions: foreign payroll, valid medical cover, and truthful renewal disclosures. If you sell, budget for capital gains rules and non-resident withholding.

Work only for foreign employers or clients unless you hold a separate work permit. Border officials and renewal officers treat permit breaches seriously.
