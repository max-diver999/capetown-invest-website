## What foreign buyers mean by property tax in South Africa

Searchers asking about property tax South Africa foreigner or foreigner property tax South Africa usually want one honest number before they offer on a Sea Point apartment or a Constantia home. South Africa does not use a single annual property tax label the way some US states do. Instead, tax on property ownership arrives in layers: a national tax at transfer, a municipal rates bill every month, income tax if you earn rent, and capital gains tax when you sell.

The good news for foreign investors is structural. South Africa does not charge a foreign-buyer stamp duty premium. Unlike Singapore, parts of Australia, or several European resort markets, a non-resident pays the same transfer duty brackets and the same City of Cape Town rates tariff as a citizen. What changes is compliance: registering with SARS as a non-resident landlord, filing provisional tax if your rental liability crosses the threshold, keeping exchange-control records so after-tax rent and sale proceeds can leave the country lawfully, and coordinating home-country tax if you remain UK or EU tax resident.

This page is a hub, not a law textbook. It summarizes each tax type, shows when it hits your cash flow, and links to dedicated guides for the full SARS tables, deduction lists, CGT base-cost rules, and HMRC Foreign Tax Credit Relief mechanics. Start here for orientation, then drill into the deep guides before you sign an Offer to Purchase or appoint a tenant.

## Master table: every property tax type for foreign owners

The table below is the map foreign buyers ask for most often. It covers the taxes that touch Cape Town residential property from offer to exit. Conveyancing and bond registration fees are included because buyers often bucket them with tax; they are professional and bank charges, not SARS or municipal taxes.

| Tax type | Who pays | When | Foreigner note |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Transfer duty (resale) | Buyer | Before Deeds Office registration; within six months of sale agreement | Same SARS brackets as locals; R327,356 on R5,000,000 under the 2025 table. No foreign surcharge. Deep guide: [transfer duty explained](/guides/south-africa-transfer-duty-explained/). |
| VAT (new build) | Built into developer price | At staged payments per OTP | 15% VAT replaces transfer duty; never both. Confirm VAT status in writing. |
| Municipal rates (Cape Town) | Owner | Monthly from month after registration | Same 0.69c/R tariff after R450k exempt band; ~R31,400/year on R5m. Deductible against rent. Deep guide: [Cape Town rates](/guides/cape-town-rates-taxes-property/). |
| Body corporate levy | Sectional title owner | Monthly | Not a tax; deductible against rental income. Budget R2,500 to R6,000/month in prime blocks. |
| Income tax on rental profit | Landlord | Annual return; provisional tax if threshold crossed | SA source tax on net rent regardless of residence. Register IT77. Deep guide: [non-resident rental tax](/guides/non-resident-rental-income-tax-south-africa/). |
| Home-country income tax (e.g. UK) | Owner if tax resident abroad | Per home-country year | Worldwide rules for UK residents; treaty credits for SA tax paid. Deep guide: [UK tax on SA rent](/guides/uk-tax-south-africa-rental-property/). |
| Capital gains tax (CGT) | Seller | Tax year of disposal; withholding at transfer | 40% inclusion for individuals; R2m primary residence exclusion rarely fits pure investments. 7.5% withholding above R2m sale price for non-residents. Deep guide: [CGT on property](/guides/south-africa-capital-gains-tax-property/). |
| CGT withholding (section 35A) | Buyer withholds; seller bears | At sale registration above R2,000,000 | 7.5% of price held as advance; reconciled on final CGT. Not the same as rental withholding. |

Two planning principles follow from this table. First, once-off purchase tax (transfer duty or VAT) is the largest single cheque for most resale buyers, and it must be paid before you own the asset. Second, ongoing holding taxes (rates plus income tax on rent) determine whether your net yield matches the gross yield on the listing. Foreign buyers who model only transfer duty and forget rates and rental tax routinely overstate cash-on-cash returns.

## Transfer duty and VAT at purchase: same rules for foreigners

Transfer duty is the national tax that dominates resale budgets. It is progressive, calculated on the purchase price in the Offer to Purchase, and paid to SARS through your conveyancer before the Deeds Office registers the property in your name. Under the table effective 1 April 2025, the first R1,210,000 of value pays zero duty, then marginal bands climb to 13% on the highest slice.

Foreign nationality does not move you into a different row. A German, British, or American buyer on a R5,000,000 Camps Bay resale pays R327,356 in transfer duty, the same figure as a South African citizen. The foreign-buyer differences sit elsewhere: introducing purchase funds through an authorised dealer, securing the non-resident endorsement on the title deed, and the roughly 50% local bond ceiling if you finance part of the price. For bracket-by-bracket math, zero-rated entry stock, and VAT-versus-duty traps on new builds, read the full [South Africa transfer duty explained](/guides/south-africa-transfer-duty-explained/) guide.

On new developments, a VAT-registered developer sells with 15% VAT already embedded in the list price. The buyer does not pay transfer duty on top. Treat any agent who says "no duty because it is new" as a prompt to verify VAT registration in the sale documents, not as a finished answer. The [cost of buying property in Cape Town](/guides/cost-of-buying-property-cape-town/) guide compares resale duty stacks against VAT-inclusive off-plan prices on the same budget.

## Municipal rates and levies: the monthly property tax foreigners feel

Cape Town property rates are a municipal charge, not a SARS line. The City of Cape Town bills residential owners on municipal valuation, not necessarily the price you paid, after exempting the first R450,000 of value for homes. The residential tariff for the 2025/2026 period is roughly 0.69 cents per rand of rateable value per year, collected in twelve monthly instalments.

On a R5,000,000 home where municipal value matches purchase price, annual rates land near R31,400, about R2,617 per month. Sectional title owners pay rates on their unit plus a body corporate levy; freehold owners pay rates on the whole erf with no levy but carry full maintenance. Foreign owners pay identical rates to locals. There is no non-resident surcharge on the municipal bill.

Rates matter twice in a foreigner's tax picture. They are an ongoing holding cost that shrinks net rental yield, and they are generally deductible against rental income when you let the property, alongside levies, insurance, and agent fees. Before you offer, ask the seller for the latest rates account and confirm the municipal valuation against the asking price. Full formulas, R2m comparisons, and sectional title versus freehold notes sit in the [Cape Town rates and taxes](/guides/cape-town-rates-taxes-property/) guide.

## Rental income tax: what SARS expects from non-resident landlords

If you let your Cape Town property, rental profit is South African source income. SARS taxes the net amount after allowable deductions, not the gross rent tenants pay. Non-residents must register for an income tax reference, typically through IT77 or the current SARS equivalent, file annual returns, and pay provisional tax in August and February if their liability crosses the registration threshold.

Allowable deductions commonly include municipal rates, body corporate levies, insurance, repairs and maintenance, letting agent commission, advertising, and bond interest on finance used to acquire the property. Capital improvements adjust base cost on eventual sale rather than wiping out rental tax in the year you spend the money. Keep every invoice in rand with proof of payment; SARS audits non-resident landlords who deduct expenses without documentation.

South Africa does not require tenants to withhold income tax on residential rent the way some countries do. You declare and pay through SARS. That is separate from the 7.5% CGT withholding on property sales above R2,000,000, which buyers handle at transfer. The full registration checklist, provisional tax workflow, expense table, and remittance through exchange control are in the [non-resident rental income tax](/guides/non-resident-rental-income-tax-south-africa/) guide.

### Rental tax outline on a typical R5m Sea Point let

The outline below models a foreign owner who bought a R5,000,000 sectional-title apartment and lets it long-term. Figures are directional for 2026 planning, not a quote for any specific unit.

| Line | Monthly (Rand) | Annual (Rand) |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Gross rent | R35,000 | R420,000 |
| Less vacancy at 8% | R2,800 | R33,600 |
| Less body corporate levy | R4,000 | R48,000 |
| Less municipal rates | R2,617 | R31,400 |
| Less insurance | R700 | R8,400 |
| Less maintenance reserve | R1,400 | R16,800 |
| Less letting agent at 10% | R3,500 | R42,000 |
| Net rental profit (pre-tax) | R19,983 | R239,800 |

On roughly R240,000 net profit, a non-resident individual might owe R45,000 to R75,000 in South African income tax depending on other SA income and available rebates. That after-tax cash is what you may remit offshore through an authorised dealer, subject to [exchange control rules](/guides/south-africa-exchange-control-property/). Underestimating this line is how a modeled 7% net yield becomes 4% in the bank.

## UK and home-country tax: second layer for some foreign owners

South African rental tax is only half the picture for UK tax residents. HMRC taxes worldwide income, including Cape Town rent, while SARS taxes the same profit as local-source income. The SA-UK double tax treaty coordinates the two: you generally pay South African tax first, then claim Foreign Tax Credit Relief on your UK Self Assessment to avoid taxing the same rand profit twice in full.

Non-UK-resident owners are usually outside UK income tax on foreign rental profit, though South African tax still applies. Residence turns on facts and days, not passport alone. Split-year treatment, remittance basis, and strong UK ties can change the answer. The dedicated [UK tax on South Africa rental property](/guides/uk-tax-south-africa-rental-property/) guide walks through Self Assessment, the property income allowance, and CGT on disposal for UK residents without repeating SARS mechanics here.

EU owners follow a similar pattern: local SA tax first, then home-country rules and applicable treaties. Always run both a South African tax practitioner and a home-country adviser before the first tenant moves in.

## Capital gains tax when you sell: exit tax for foreign owners

Capital gains tax taxes the profit when you dispose of South African immovable property. For individuals, 40% of the net capital gain is included in normal income tax and taxed at your marginal rate, up to 45% for top earners. There is no flat CGT percentage standing alone.

Base cost includes purchase price plus acquisition costs such as transfer duty, conveyancing on purchase, and capital improvements with proof. Selling costs such as agent commission reduce proceeds. The R2,000,000 primary residence exclusion applies only when the property was your main home under SARS rules, so pure holiday lets and buy-to-let stock rarely qualify. Individuals also receive an annual exclusion of R40,000 against capital gains each tax year before inclusion.

Foreign sellers pay South African CGT on the same structural rules as residents. On sales above R2,000,000, the buyer must withhold 7.5% of the purchase price as an advance against CGT for a natural-person seller. You reconcile withholding against the final liability through your tax return. After tax is settled, sale proceeds can be repatriated through an authorised dealer if purchase funds were introduced correctly. Worked base-cost math, withholding timelines, and repatriation checkpoints are in the [South Africa CGT on property](/guides/south-africa-capital-gains-tax-property/) guide.

## Worked example: foreign buyer, R5 million resale, full tax life cycle

The scenario below ties the hub together. A UK-based buyer purchases a R5,000,000 resale sectional-title apartment in Sea Point for investment, lets it long-term, and sells five years later. Numbers use 2025/2026 tables and directional market assumptions. Confirm every line with your conveyancer and tax advisers before you rely on them.

Profile: Non-resident individual, cash purchase, no primary residence claim, UK tax resident who files Self Assessment.

### At purchase (month zero)

| Cost or tax line | Amount (Rand) | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Purchase price | R5,000,000 | Resale from private seller |
| Transfer duty | R327,356 | Same for foreign and local buyer |
| Conveyancing and Deeds Office (approx.) | R48,000 to R52,000 | Professional fees, not tax |
| Total once-off above price | ~R375,000 to R380,000 | Duty dominates the stack |

Transfer duty calculation on R5,000,000: fixed R106,784 for the band above R2,994,800, plus 11% of R2,005,200 = R220,572, total R327,356. See the [transfer duty guide](/guides/south-africa-transfer-duty-explained/) for the full bracket walk-through.

### During ownership (years one to five)

| Ongoing line | Annual (Rand) | Tax treatment |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Municipal rates | ~R31,400 | Same tariff as locals; deductible against rent |
| Body corporate levy | ~R48,000 | Deductible against rent |
| SA income tax on net rent | ~R45,000 to R75,000 | On ~R240,000 net profit after expenses |
| UK tax top-up (if any) | Depends on bracket | FTC relief for SA tax paid |

Monthly rates of R2,617 continue regardless of occupancy. If the flat sits empty between tenants, you still pay rates and levies while earning no offsetting rent, which is why vacancy assumptions belong in every foreign buyer's model. Align SARS registration with the [FICA files](/guides/fica-requirements-foreign-property-buyers/) you already supplied at purchase.

### At sale (year five, illustrative)

Assume sale price R6,200,000, agent commission 5%, and no major capital improvements beyond maintenance.

| CGT building block | Amount (Rand) |
| --- | --- |
| Sale price | R6,200,000 |
| Less agent commission | R310,000 |
| Proceeds | R5,890,000 |
| Less base cost (price + duty + purchase costs) | R5,380,000 |
| Capital gain before exclusions | R510,000 |
| Less annual exclusion | R40,000 |
| Net capital gain | R470,000 |
| Taxable portion at 40% inclusion | R188,000 |
| Indicative CGT at 31% marginal rate | ~R58,000 |
| Buyer withholding at 7.5% of price | R465,000 held; reconciled to final CGT |

Withholding feels large against the final CGT bill because it is calculated on price, not gain. Your tax practitioner reconciles the difference after transfer. Repatriation of net proceeds follows exchange-control rules covered in the [foreign buyer hub](/guides/buy-cape-town-property-foreigner/) and [exchange control guide](/guides/south-africa-exchange-control-property/).

## Calendar: when each tax hits your bank account

Foreign owners stay compliant when they know which authority bills when. Use this timeline alongside the master table.

| Timing | Tax or charge | Authority | Action |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Before registration | Transfer duty or VAT | SARS / developer | Conveyancer pays duty; OTP confirms VAT status |
| Monthly from transfer | Municipal rates | City of Cape Town | Pay or debit order; deduct if letting |
| Monthly | Body corporate levy | Managing agent | Deduct against rental income |
| August and February | Provisional tax (if registered) | SARS | Pay estimated rental tax |
| After tax year-end | Annual income tax return | SARS | Declare net rent; reconcile provisional payments |
| 31 January (UK) | Self Assessment if UK resident | HMRC | Declare SA rent; claim FTC relief |
| Year of sale | CGT and withholding | SARS / conveyancer | Withholding at transfer; file final return |

Missing a filing date hurts more than missing a spreadsheet row. SARS penalties and interest on undeclared rent, and HMRC late-filing fines, compound quietly while the property appreciates.

## Red flags before you commit

- An agent quotes "10% all-in tax" without separating transfer duty, rates, and rental tax.
- A new build marketed as "no transfer duty" without confirming VAT registration in the OTP.
- No request for municipal valuation or latest rates account before offer.
- Letting starts before SARS registration or without a tax practitioner engaged.
- UK resident assumes treaty relief without filing Self Assessment or keeping SA assessments.
- Sale proceeds planned offshore without exchange-control records from the original purchase.

Cash UK investor, R5m Sea Point flat, long-term let: Budget R327,356 duty at entry, R31,400/year rates, R48,000/year levy, and R45,000 to R75,000/year SA rental tax on modeled net rent, plus UK reporting with FTC relief. EU cash buyer, R2.8m Century City apartment, occasional personal use: Lower duty near R106,784, rates near R16,000/year, rental tax only on let months. CGT primary residence exclusion unlikely if let most of the year.

Non-resident financing 50% locally: Duty still calculated on full R5m price, not equity brought from abroad. Bond interest deductible against rent; bond registration costs are not tax but affect cash flow.
