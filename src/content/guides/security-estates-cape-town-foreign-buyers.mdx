There is no visa requirement to own and no foreign-buyer surcharge.

A thorough estate due diligence covers:

1. **HOA financial statements.** Ask for the last two to three years. A healthy association runs a surplus, maintains a funded reserve, and is not leaning on debt.
2. **The reserve fund.** This is the savings pot for major future works such as roads, roofs and security upgrades. A thin reserve is a red flag, because the shortfall lands on owners as special levies.
3. **Levy history and increases.** Steady, inflation-tracking increases are normal. Sharp jumps or a string of special levies suggest underfunding or poor planning.
4. **The conduct rules and constitution.** Read what you can and cannot do, especially around letting, renovations and pets, so there are no surprises after transfer.
5. **Special levies, raised or planned.** Ask directly whether any special levy is in discussion. A pending levy for a major project can add tens of thousands of rand to your first year.
6. **Management and security track record.** Who runs the estate, is it self-managed or professionally managed, and what is the security provider's record?

A well-run estate with a healthy reserve and disciplined management protects your capital and is the whole point of paying the levy. A poorly run one, with deferred maintenance and reactive special levies, can quietly erode value no matter how nice the individual house is.
