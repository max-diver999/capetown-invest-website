faq:
  - question: "What is a body corporate in Cape Town sectional title?"
    answer: "A body corporate is the legal entity comprising all owners in a sectional title scheme. It maintains common property, sets levies, enforces conduct rules, and holds reserve funds. When you buy an apartment, you inherit its financial health and rules — not just the interior of your unit."
  - question: "What body corporate documents should I request before buying?"
    answer: "Request the latest audited financial statements, management accounts for the current year, the approved budget, reserve fund plan, schedule of levies for the unit, certificate of compliance where applicable, conduct rules, and minutes from the last two AGMs including any special levy resolutions. Your conveyancer should obtain a levy clearance certificate before transfer."
  - question: "What is a special levy and why does it matter?"
    answer: "A special levy is an additional charge beyond the monthly levy, usually voted at a general meeting to fund unexpected repairs — lift replacement, facade waterproofing, or fire compliance. A pending R80,000 special levy on a R2 million apartment destroys net yield. Search AGM minutes for upcoming projects before you offer."
  - question: "Can I run Airbnb in any Cape Town sectional title building?"
    answer: "No. Body corporate conduct rules often restrict or ban short-term letting. Even where rules are silent, City of Cape Town by-laws apply. If your investment thesis is short-stay income, read registered conduct rules and recent enforcement letters before reservation — not after transfer."
  - question: "What levy level is too high for investment?"
    answer: "There is no universal cap — compare levy per square metre against similar buildings in the same suburb. If monthly levy exceeds about 30% of achievable long-let rent on the unit, net yield usually compresses below suburb averages. Always model net yield with the actual levy on the schedule, not a developer forecast."
  - question: "Do foreign buyers face different body corporate rules?"
    answer: "No. Foreign owners face the same conduct rules, levies, and voting rights as local owners once registered. Non-resident status affects exchange control and tax, not body corporate membership. FICA and levy clearance still run through the conveyancer at transfer."

## Quick answer: why body corporate diligence saves deals

In Cape Town most foreign-friendly stock is sectional title — City Bowl towers, Sea Point apartments, [Century City](/guides/century-city-property-investment-guide/) units. You buy membership in a shared balance sheet. A R2.5 million one-bedroom with a R4,500 levy and no special levies is a different investment from an identical unit paying R9,000 with a R120,000 lift levy pending.

This guide is the checklist STR investors, offshore buyers, and semigration purchasers use before suspensive conditions expire.

## Documents to request (minimum pack)

| Document | What you learn |
| --- | --- |
| Audited financials (2 years) | Surplus vs deficit culture |
| Current management accounts | Runway and arrears |
| Approved annual budget | Levy trend next 12 months |
| Reserve fund plan (STSM Act) | Legal minimum vs actual |
| Levy schedule for unit | Exact monthly charge |
| Conduct rules (registered) | Pets, STR, alterations |
| AGM minutes (2 years) | Special levies, disputes |
| Insurance schedule | Common property cover |
| CSOS complaints (if any) | Active warfare |

Your conveyancer should obtain levy clearance before registration — but investment logic needs the pack before offer.

## Special levies and reserve fund red flags

Special levy triggers:

- Lift modernization (common in 1970s CBD towers)
- Facade crack repairs on Atlantic Seaboard salt exposure
- Fire compliance upgrades post-insurance audit
- Pool pump failure in amenity-heavy buildings

AGM minutes phrases to fear:

- “Special levy approved…”
- “Quote pending for structural…”
- “Insurance claim rejected…”
- “Trustee resignation / CSOS referral…”

Reserve fund: Sectional Titles Schemes Management Act requires 10-year maintenance plans. Weak reserves predict future shocks.

## Short-term rental and conduct rules

City of Cape Town by-laws plus body corporate rules stack:

| Layer | Can ban STR? |
| --- | --- |
| City zoning / by-law | Permitted zones with registration |
| Conduct rules | Often stricter — total ban |
| Trustee enforcement | Fines and legal action |

If thesis is Airbnb, read rules before offer — not after transfer.

Guides: [short-term rental rules](/guides/short-term-rental-rules-cape-town/), [Airbnb investment Cape Town](/guides/airbnb-investment-cape-town-guide/).

Many [Blok](/developers/blok-urban-developers/) and hotel-hybrid schemes use managed letting — different contract, not standard STR.

## Trustee governance and arrears

Healthy schemes show:

- Arrears under 10% of annual budget (rule of thumb)
- Professional managing agent with monthly reports
- Contested but minuted AGMs — silence can hide conflict

Unhealthy signals:

- Self-managed with no audits
- Same trustee for 15 years with no transparency
- Owners litigating over parking bay allocations

---

## Parking, exclusive use, and storage

Levy schedules often exclude:

- Second parking bay (R500–R1,500+ extra monthly)
- Storage cage
- Marina-style boat parking (rare)

Match exclusive use areas on sectional plan to what you inspect.

- Same conduct rules apply — no exemption.

- Non-resident endorsement unrelated to body corporate — see [foreign buyer hub](/guides/buy-cape-town-property-foreigner/).
- Remote buyers should use local agent or conveyancer to obtain BC pack — POA does not remove DD.

Cons

- Levy inflation and special levies
- STR often banned
- Trustee politics

### Conveyancer and agent workflow

Ask your conveyancer to request the body corporate pack in the offer-to-purchase as a suspensive condition — typical window 7 to 14 business days. Agents in Cape Town often have PDF packs on file; insist on full audited sets, not marketing summaries.

Compare levy per square metre: divide monthly levy by unit size on the sectional plan. A R4,200 levy on 55 m² equals R76/m²; on 85 m² equals R49/m² — same headline levy, different economics.

When buying in [Harbour Arch](/projects/harbour-arch-amdec-foreshore/) or [Foreshore Place](/projects/foreshore-place-cape-town/), parking bays may be separate levy lines — add R800 to R1,800 monthly per bay in some towers.

Trustees must provide levy clearance before transfer; arrears by prior owner should not attach to you if clearance is correct — still verify with managing agent on registration day.

For STR investors, cross-reference City registration requirements in [short-term rental rules](/guides/short-term-rental-rules-cape-town/) even when body corporate is silent — municipal law may still constrain nights per year.

## Sample due diligence timeline

| Day | Action |
| --- | --- |
| 1 | Request BC pack from agent |
| 3 | Review audited statements and AGM minutes |
| 5 | Model net yield with actual levy |
| 7 | Confirm conduct rules on STR and pets |
| 10 | Suspensive condition satisfied or walk away |

Insider tip: phone the managing agent directly — not only the seller's agent — and ask whether any special levy vote is scheduled before transfer. Verbal confirmation plus emailed minutes beats silence.

Older Sea Point towers built 1970–1985 often carry lift maintenance funds below replacement cost; ask for the last lift service contract value if the building exceeds 12 storeys.

When comparing [Century City](/guides/century-city-property-investment-guide/) schemes, the Century City Property Owners' Association layer sits above individual body corporates — request both levy stacks.

Water recovery charges after Cape Town drought-era retrofits appear as separate lines on some levies — R200 to R450 monthly — easy to miss in headline marketing.

When you inherit arrears disputes between trustees and owners, ask whether CSOS has an open file — unresolved disputes can delay levy clearance and push transfer past your bond grant expiry.

For foreign buyers using [foreign buyer hub](/guides/buy-cape-town-property-foreigner/) mechanics, remember levy clearance is still a local transfer requirement; non-resident endorsement on title does not waive body corporate compliance.

Developers marketing forecast levies on off-plan sales in 2026 often quote year-one budgets 15% to 25% below stabilized costs once lifts, pools, and security contracts renew — stress-test year-three levy in your model.

Closing verification checklist: audited financials received, special levy search complete, conduct rules read, net yield rebuilt, managing agent interviewed, levy clearance path confirmed with conveyancer, and long-let fallback modeled if STR is banned.

Request the sectional title plan showing exclusive use areas — parking, storage, and balcony boundaries often explain levy differences between otherwise identical units on the same floor.

If the scheme shares a pool you will not use, you still pay — compare levy per owner against buildings without wet amenities when yield is the goal.
