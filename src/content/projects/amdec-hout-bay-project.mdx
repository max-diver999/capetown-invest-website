## Cape Town Invest lens on Hout Bay harbour stock

Portfolio #24 implied an Amdec project in Hout Bay. Amdec's published Western Cape pipeline is **Harbour Arch** on the Foreshore — a six-tower precinct with about 1,500 apartments, not a harbour village scheme. Cape Town Invest uses slug `amdec-hout-bay-project` to redirect Amdec searchers honestly while documenting **La'Mare** as the verified off-plan anchor on Bayview Road.

Hout Bay offers Atlantic Seaboard lifestyle without Camps Bay tickets: harbour restaurants, Chapman's Peak access, and semigration families who accept a 25-minute CBD commute. It is not a pure yield market — compare MODELED numbers against [Woodstock](/projects/acacia-house-woodstock/) income stock before you buy lifestyle brochures.

Link [Amdec](/developers/amdec-property-investments/) for CBD gateway exposure and the [Atlantic Seaboard guide](/guides/atlantic-seaboard-property-investment-guide/) for prime-tier context.

## Off-plan diligence checklist

1. NHBRC enrolment certificate for the scheme.
2. Deposit held in trust or bank guarantee per sale agreement.
3. Build programme milestones with penalty clauses.
4. Estimated levy schedule from developer — stress +20% versus brochure.
5. Conduct rules draft on short-stay and Airbnb.
6. View and noise simulation for harbour restaurant strip.

## Yield and letting on the Atlantic fringe

Hout Bay long-let tenants include families, harbour workers, and remote professionals who want mountain-sea lifestyle. MODELED gross near 5% to 6% on R2 million to R3 million tickets is realistic before levies; net nearer 3.5% to 4.5% after rates and voids.

Short-stay marketing often cites summer peaks. Rebuild with 20% management, body corporate night caps, and two winter void months unless proven otherwise.

Compare [Camps Bay](/guides/camps-bay-property-investment/) if capital preservation beats harbour value entry.

## Hout Bay market context versus Camps Bay

Hout Bay median tickets sit materially below Camps Bay village while sharing Atlantic Seaboard postcode psychology. Semigration buyers who cannot clear R8 million Camps Bay entry often start harbour searches here — supporting resale liquidity on handover if build quality holds.

Chapman's Peak drive time to the CBD stretches in peak hour — underwrite tenants who work locally or remote, not five-day CBD commuters unless they accept congestion cost.

## Bond and completion cash flow

Foreign buyers often fund 50% offshore and bond 50% on registration at handover. Model interim deposit schedule plus transfer duty at completion — cash trap risk rises if handover slips beyond Q3 2027.

Stage payments should tie to verifiable construction milestones — visit site quarterly if you live offshore. Photos from the developer are not a substitute for engineer sign-off on structure and waterproofing at harbour level.

---

## Long-let versus short-stay at harbour

Harbour restaurants generate weekend foot traffic — good for short-stay ADR, noisy for long-let tenants. Read draft conduct rules before you assume Airbnb is allowed in your block. Hout Bay body corporates sometimes cap guest nights below City Bowl towers.

Compare handover timing against [Observatory pipeline](/projects/observatory-green/) — different tenant pool, similar semigration narrative.

## Scenario workbook for Hout Bay off-plan

Base case: R2.4m two-bedroom, R14,000 long-let, R3,500 levy at handover, 11-month occupancy — net near 4.2% in year two.

Delay case: six-month handover slip — carry bond and rates on unfinished unit; stress in cash flow.

Resale case: sell on handover to lifestyle buyer at 10% premium — plausible in strong semigration years, not guaranteed.

Before you reserve La'Mare off-plan, verify NHBRC enrolment, deposit beneficiary, build milestone penalties, projected levy schedule, short-stay rules, and flood/engineering sign-off for harbour-edge construction. Amdec buyers belong on Harbour Arch — not on a Hout Bay portal alias.
