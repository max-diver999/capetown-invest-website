## Cape Town Invest lens on Nine Palms Century City

Nine Palms matters because it was the demand signal that unlocked Rabie's Ratanga Park residential pipeline. Before [On Park](/projects/on-park-century-city/) and before the canal-facing [SkyWater](/projects/skywater-century-city/) launch, Nine Palms proved that buyers would absorb more than 130 units on the park edge at record pace. Rabie's own commentary notes record sales at launch and semigration-driven demand from across South Africa and the continent, which is exactly the rental depth foreign investors want to see before they buy sectional title far from the Atlantic Seaboard.

For an international buyer, Nine Palms is now a completed-stock play, not an off-plan story. That shifts the due diligence from brochure projections to audited body corporate accounts, actual levy trends, and live rental comparables on Property24 and through local letting agents. The scheme targeted EDGE certification from the Green Building Council of South Africa, which can support lower running costs and tenant appeal, but you should confirm the final certification status on the unit you target.

## Park-front positioning and rental demand

Ratanga Park is Rabie's newer mixed-use node inside Century City, designed to add residential frontage to water and green space rather than only canal or office exposure. Nine Palms sits on the park edge with a large private garden retained from the precinct's original landscaping story, including heritage palm planting that gave the scheme its name. That park adjacency supports weekend lifestyle marketing and weekday commuter convenience to Canal Walk, the CBD, and the N1.

Tenant demand in Century City is driven by corporate professionals, Canal Walk and Century City office workers, and semigration families who want suburban security with city access. Nine Palms one-bedroom units around 50 square metres trade on portals in the high-R2-millions, which implies gross yields in the 7% band if monthly rent sits in the R14,000 to R17,000 range, but you must verify live asking rent for the exact stack and aspect before you model.

Long-let is the base case for foreign owners who cannot self-manage. Short-let is possible in some Century City blocks but adds regulation and body corporate rule risk, so underwrite long-let first.

## Resale economics: transfer duty versus off-plan VAT

Nine Palms primary sales were VAT-inclusive off-plan transactions from a registered developer, with no transfer duty on the initial purchase. Resale today follows the SARS transfer duty table on the purchase price, plus conveyancing and Deeds Office fees. On a R2,750,000 resale, transfer duty alone is roughly R89,000, and conveyancing with VAT can add another R28,000 to R32,000, so your true entry cost is about 4% to 5% above the listing price before bond costs.

Work the comparison using our [cost of buying guide](/guides/cost-of-buying-property-cape-town/) worksheet. The question is not whether Nine Palms is cheaper than off-plan SkyWater nominally, but whether the completed levy, actual rent, and park aspect justify the premium over generic Milnerton stock on a net-yield basis.

## Pros, cons, and who Nine Palms suits

**Pros**

- Completed occupied stock with real rental and levy data available.
- Park-front address inside a master-planned, security-managed precinct.
- Rabie track record and strong initial sell-out signal.
- Income-led MODELED yields competitive with Cape Town coastal entry pricing.
- No foreign buyer surcharge; local bond available up to 50% for non-residents.

**Cons**

- Primary stock closed; you compete on resale pricing.
- Transfer duty and conveyancing add to entry cost versus original off-plan VAT pricing.
- Further Rabie phases add supply that can soften rents.
- Sectional title governance determines real net yield more than the developer name.

**The Century City income investor** wants park or canal adjacency with professional tenant demand and MODELED net yield above 5%. Nine Palms fits if net math works after real levy.

**The semigration landlord** targets long-let to relocating families and professionals; verify school and commute patterns from Milnerton.

**The foreign first-time buyer** uses Nine Palms as a completed proxy for Rabie quality before risking off-plan elsewhere; read the [foreign buyer hub](/guides/buy-cape-town-property-foreigner/) and confirm FICA paths.

**The resale flip buyer** should note Rabie commentary about profitable re-sales shortly after occupation in Ratanga Park; treat that as market colour, not a promise, and model holding costs conservatively.

## Nine Palms red flags

- Resale priced like off-plan VAT without adding transfer duty and conveyancing to your worksheet.
- Gross yield quoted from peak summer short-let without a long-let fallback model.
- Body corporate financials older than 12 months on a scheme still settling owner mix.
- Ignoring new supply from On Park and SkyWater when underwriting rent growth.
- Special levy risk not reviewed in trustee minutes before offer.
