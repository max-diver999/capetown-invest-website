## Cape Town Invest lens on Harbour Arch

Portfolio slug `harbour-arch` often appears on aggregator sites with vague developer labels. The verified investable product is No. 1 Harbour Arch — Amdec Tower 1 on the Culemborg/Foreshore site — not a generic CBD listing. Amdec exported the Melrose Arch mixed-use playbook to Cape Town: apartments above retail, hotel keys nearby, parking basements measured in thousands of bays, and placemaking around an urban park and galleria that will mature as later towers complete.

Tower 1 handed over from May 2023 after construction that started in February 2020, surviving Covid-era supply disruption. That delivery record matters when you compare Harbour Arch against unproven off-plan sponsors elsewhere on the Foreshore. Pricing from about R1.8 million on studios places Amdec between [Foreshore Place](/projects/foreshore-place-cape-town/) conversion resale and ultra-prime Waterfront tickets.

Foreign semigration buyers and corporate long-stay investors shortlist Harbour Arch for highway access, CTICC proximity, and walkability toward the [City Bowl](/guides/cape-town-city-bowl-property-investment/). Yield hunters should stress-test levies — parking, pool, and precinct security load the body corporate — before they assume Atlantic Seaboard rent bands.

## Precinct layout and phasing

Harbour Arch occupies roughly 5.8 hectares on the Foreshore — the former Culemborg site — with Christiaan Barnard Street as the gateway spine. The master plan targets six towers, about 1,500 residential apartments, roughly 200,000 m² of offices, two Marriott-branded hotels with about 500 keys, retail galleria, an eighth-floor open-air piazza, urban park, and flagship fitness club.

Tower 1 mixes residential floors with ground-floor restaurants and high-end vehicle retail. Later towers follow as leasing and sales absorb Tower 1 inventory — Amdec has publicly framed Tower 2 as the next phase once Tower 1 stabilises. Investors buying today own into a precinct that will gain foot traffic and amenity depth as the skyline fills in, but also face construction noise and competing supply within the same brand.

## Unit mix and floor plans

Developer marketing lists studios from 35 m² through three-bedroom layouts to 109 m², with types labelled A through F on the sales site. Smaller studios target young professionals, post-graduates, and corporate long-stay operators; larger two- and three-bedroom corners suit semigration owner-occupiers who want Foreshore access without suburban commuting.

Harbour Arch Property Sales resale listings in 2026 illustrate live pricing: harbour-facing 35 m² studios around R1.9 million, 51 m² one-beds near R2.8 million, 73 m² to 86 m² two-beds from about R3.25 million to R4.7 million, and 109 m² three-beds up to about R6 million. ## Amenities and levy stack

Tower 1 markets a sun deck, outdoor pool, secure basement and above-ground parking, controlled electronic access, CCTV, and upscale finishes across the precinct. Those amenities support long-stay corporate demand but inflate levies relative to plain walk-up CBD stock.

Request two years of audited body corporate financials, the parking bay allocation (included vs additional purchase), and any special levy history tied to pool, lift, or fire-compliance upgrades. Parking at Harbour Arch is a material cost line — about 1,200 bays serve 560 apartments, but not every unit includes a bay in base price.

## How do foreign buyers complete a Harbour Arch purchase?

Non-residents buy Harbour Arch like any sectional title product by identifying primary versus resale because developer stock may be VAT-inclusive while resale attracts transfer duty, offering with suspensive bond clause if financing locally up to about 50 percent LTV, completing FICA through conveyancer with offshore funds via authorised dealer, reviewing NHBRC coverage on remaining primary stock or body corporate health on resale, and registering non-resident endorsement for repatriation on exit.
