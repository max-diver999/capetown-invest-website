## Cape Town Invest lens on Woodstock Albert Road

Portfolio #22 listed Acacia House in Woodstock with Blok attribution. A full scan of [Blok](/developers/blok-urban-developers/) developments shows **zero Woodstock schemes** — the studio's map stops at Atlantic Seaboard and De Waterkant. Generic portals reuse Acacia House naming from unrelated assets (office parks, student housing) that are not investable sectional title in Cape Town.

Cape Town Invest keeps slug `acacia-house-woodstock` to capture that search intent honestly. The verified anchors are **WEX1** on Albert Road and **The Iron Works** in Upper Woodstock: mixed-use, completed stock where investors underwrite levies, short-let rules, and walkability to the CBD.

Woodstock is the creative-industrial fringe that gentrified ahead of the [Observatory](/projects/observatory-green/) student corridor. Read the [Woodstock investment guide](/guides/woodstock-property-investment/) and [City Bowl guide](/guides/cape-town-city-bowl-property-investment/) for macro yields before you pick a building.

## The Iron Works: Upper Woodstock resale

Land Equity Group delivered The Iron Works around 2019 with roughly 136 units from R1.3 million to R2.8 million including VAT at launch. One- and two-bedroom layouts of 50 to 106 m² targeted young professionals and creative tenants, with marketed rentals from R10,500 per month.

Upper Woodstock sits above the Albert Road strip with views toward Table Mountain and quicker access to the M3. Compare levies per square metre against WEX1 before you assume Albert Road always wins — Iron Works can offer lower entry with similar tenant depth.

## Yield and tenant profile

Woodstock tenants include design studios, remote workers, and students spilling from [Observatory](/projects/observatory-green/). MODELED gross near 7% to 8% is achievable on well-priced two-bedroom stock if voids stay under one month annually.

Rebuild net with:

- Body corporate levy trend (mixed-use blocks often run R2,500 to R4,500 on 80 m²).
- Rates on City of Cape Town accounts.
- 8% to 10% rental agent fee on long-let.
- Short-stay management near 20% if body corporate allows it.

Contrast with [Foreshore Place](/projects/foreshore-place-cape-town/) if you want newer tower stock with mall adjacency — tickets and levies differ even though both are City Bowl fringe.

- Lower entry than Atlantic Seaboard.
- Walkable to CBD and East City design district.
- Completed stock with rental history.
- No foreign buyer surcharge.
- Mixed-use amenity at WEX1.

**Cons**

- No Blok design premium here.
- Gentrification already priced in on Albert Road.
- Mixed-use levy and conduct complexity.
- Parking and load-shedding vary by scheme.
- Not a trophy capital-preservation play.

The income investor wants two-bedroom stock with proven leases under R2.5 million.

The value-add buyer renovates Iron Works units below strip average per m².

The foreign buyer records funds and compares levy per m² across WEX1 and Iron Works before offer.

## Albert Road amenity and tenant demand

The Old Biscuit Mill and East City design district feed weekday lunch traffic and weekend markets — a tenant amenity WEX1 owners market to remote workers. Verify whether your unit's levy subsidises hotel common areas disproportionately versus pure residential blocks.

Iron Works tenants often work in Woodstock creative offices or commute via the N2. Parking bay allocation affects rentability — confirm exclusive use rights on the title schedule.

---

## Transfer duty and VAT on resale

WEX1 and Iron Works resale attract transfer duty on the SARS sliding scale. If any developer primary stock remains, confirm VAT-inclusive pricing and whether transfer duty is absent on first sale — unlikely on 2019 completions but worth asking on leftover inventory.

Woodstock capital growth tied the suburb to the City Bowl tech and design economy — tenant demand can soften if remote workers relocate. Keep six months of levy and rates in reserve when voids extend.

---

## Rental agent and tenant vetting

Use agents who know Woodstock blocks individually — generic City Bowl averages misprice Iron Works walk-up units versus WEX1 lift access. Credit-check tenants and record deposit in trust per Rental Housing Act.

---

## Scenario workbook for Woodstock apartments

Base case: long-let professional couple, R18,000 monthly rent on R2.2m purchase, R3,200 levy, 11-month occupancy — net near 5.5%.

Short-stay case: body corporate allows 90 nights, peak summer only — gross can touch 9% but net falls after fees and voids.

Resale case: 5% annual capital growth on City Bowl fringe — plausible when semigration holds; stress 0% for two years in underwriting.

## NHBRC and mixed-use body corporate risk

Mixed-use precincts can carry higher reserve fund requirements when hotel lifts and pool plant serve multiple zones. Read the ten-year maintenance plan in body corporate minutes before you assume R3,000 levies stay flat.

Signatura's WEX Living award press coverage helps resale storytelling — but awards do not guarantee levy discipline. Numbers in audited financials beat marketing.

---

## Closing verification checklist

Before you treat Woodstock resale as investment-ready, verify scheme name, levy trend, special levy history, parking bay allocation, inverter or backup power scope, and short-stay approval. Albert Road premium is in the data — not in the portal project name.

## What to verify next

Confirm building name on title (WEX1 or Iron Works, not Acacia House), pull audited body corporate financials, read short-let conduct rules, collect three Woodstock rentals, and model transfer duty via the [cost guide](/guides/cost-of-buying-property-cape-town/). Area macro: [Woodstock guide](/guides/woodstock-property-investment/). Blok stock lives on the [Atlantic Seaboard](/guides/atlantic-seaboard-property-investment-guide/), not here.

Income investors pairing Woodstock with [Foreshore Place](/projects/foreshore-place-cape-town/) should compare levy per m² and tenant profile — Foreshore suits mall-adjacent professionals; Woodstock suits creative tenants. Do not average yields across both without unit-level math.

WEX1 hotel sections may restrict owner renovations that affect common ducting — read alteration rules before you buy a “value-add” unit. Iron Works allows more typical cosmetic upgrades but check heritage overlay on Victoria Road facade elements.

Ask the body corporate for the last three years of levy increases — Woodstock blocks with 8% annual levy growth erase MODELED yield gains from rent bumps.

Pull two Iron Works and two WEX1 resale comps before you accept the first asking price on either scheme. Verify parking allocation on title.
