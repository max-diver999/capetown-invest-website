## Cape Town Invest lens on Infinity Bakoven

Portfolio #17 listed Camps Bay Infinity with POA pricing. On the ground, verified press and architect portfolios identify **Infinity** on Victoria Road in **Bakoven** — the mountainside gateway between Clifton and Camps Bay village. Cape Town Invest uses slug `camps-bay-infinity` to close that mapping and to warn against unrelated portal listings that invent Florida-registered sellers and villa counts.

Bakoven is not generic Camps Bay. It is the quieter, ultra-prime cove strip where Victoria Road hugs the mountain and the last developable ocean-view stands attracted trophy architects. Infinity marketed itself as one of the final chances to buy new luxury on that curve before the stock aged into established resale.

This is rarely a yield purchase. ## Resale underwriting on Victoria Road

Pull three Victoria Road transactions within 500 metres: Bakoven, Camps Bay village, and Bantry Bay fringe. Ultra-prime resales on the Atlantic Seaboard can sit R12 million to R35 million depending on view band and renovation age — Infinity launch pricing in 2016 anchors expectations but does not cap 2026 resale.

Model carrying cost honestly:

- Body corporate levy: pull from seller or managing agent — not published in launch-era press.
- Municipal rates on coastal accounts: often R3,000 to R8,000+ monthly at trophy size.
- Insurance on terrace pools and high wind exposure.
- Special levy risk if lift or pool plant deferred.

Subtract void months if you let seasonally. A R25 million ticket with R15,000 monthly carrying before bond service needs exceptional rent to clear 3% net — which is why buyers here accept 4% net on mainstream Camps Bay as the ceiling, not the floor.

## Victoria Road comparable sales framework

When valuing Infinity resale, weight transactions by view band: mountain-side versus ocean glimpse versus full Atlantic horizon. Bakoven pricing is non-linear — a 10% better view can justify 20% premium on the strip. Use Lightstone or deeds office transfers for Victoria Road, not generic Camps Bay medians.

Insurance underwriters scrutinise terrace pools and cliff-edge wind exposure. Budget replacement cost for glass and pool plant above standard sectional title blocks in Sea Point.

---

## Foreign buyer mechanics on trophy resale

Non-resident buyers introduce offshore capital through an authorised dealer and file with the conveyancer for exchange control. Bond advance near 50% LTV requires South African credit footprint or offshore collateral acceptable to the bank. Transfer duty on R24 million resale sits in the top SARS band — model before you negotiate.

Sellers marketing trophy Atlantic Seaboard stock often quote dollar prices — rebuild your home-currency exit at offer stage, not only at sale. Rand depreciation helped 2016 launch buyers; future repatriation depends on rates at exit.

---

## Lift, pool, and common-area replacement cycle

Six-unit buildings still face lift motor replacement every 15 to 20 years and pool plant refurbishment every 8 to 10 years. A special levy of R200,000 split six ways is material — read reserve fund balances before you unconditional.

Before you treat Infinity Bakoven resale as investment-ready, verify building name on title, body corporate levy trend, special levy history, terrace pool maintenance responsibility, short-let approval, and three Atlantic Seaboard resale comps within 500 metres. ## Scenario workbook for trophy seaboard

Base case: long-let executive tenant, 10-month occupancy, 5% levy inflation, conservative R80,000 monthly rent on a R24m purchase — gross near 4%, net nearer 2.5% after costs.

Seasonal case: December–February short-stay at premium nightly rates with 30% management fee — gross can spike but net remains single-digit unless you self-manage.

Resale case: 3% annual capital growth in USD terms requires rand stability plus Atlantic Seaboard liquidity — plausible in good cycles, not guaranteed.
